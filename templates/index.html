Pinochle Scoring & Guide

Official rules, point references, and bidding suggestions

Important Meld Rule: Melds must be held individually by a single player. Cards cannot be combined between partners/teams to form runs or marriages. Furthermore, a partnership must accumulate at least 20 points of total combined meld to claim any meld score; otherwise, their meld score is 0. Note that 9s of trumps (Dix) are not in the deck and have been removed.

Trick Scoring

Players score points from winning counters in tricks (Aces, 10s, Kings are 10 points each in single deck, but scaled down to 1 point each in the double/triple deck setup to match modern tournament scoring).

  • 4-Player Games (2-Teams): Total of 50 trick points available per round.
  • 6-Player Games (3-Teams): Total of 75 trick points available per round.

Minimum Starting Bid

  • 4-Player / 2-Teams: Minimum starting bid of 50 points.
  • 6-Player / 3-Teams: Minimum starting bid of 60 points.

The 10-Point Save Rule

To count any of their declared meld points, non-bidding partnerships must score at least 10 trick points in play during the round. If a partnership wins fewer than 10 trick points, their meld is voided and not added to their score ("meld did not save").

Going Set

The bidding partnership must score at least the amount of their bid between their meld and trick points combined. If their total points are less than the bid, they "go set". Their meld points are thrown out, and their total bid is subtracted from their score.

Determining Your Maximum Realistic Bid

Avoid over-bidding. Standard guidelines for predicting your team's maximum safe bid limit:

4-Player (Double Deck) Formula:
20 + 10 + Your Meld

20 tricks minimum + 10 points partner meld estimate + your active meld

6-Player (Triple Deck) Formula:
30 + 15 + Your Meld

30 tricks minimum + 15 points partner meld estimate + your active meld

Bidding Best Practices

  • 1. Pass early if weak: If you have no marriage (Class A sequence of King & Queen) and fewer than 15 meld points, pass early to avoid misleading your partners.
  • 2. Signal your strength: Initial bids usually signal whether you possess meld or a strong trump fit. Make incremental raises to indicate specific high-scoring card layouts.
  • 3. Assess the trump fit: A run in trump is highly valuable not just for its 15 meld points, but also for controlling all trump cards during play to capture aces.
